Neurodiversity Isle of Wight 2019

Friday 8th November 2019. Riverside Centre, Newport, Isle of Wight, PO30 2QR



About

Aimed at neurodivergent adults, parents, carers and family members of neurodivergent people, and professionals working in the field, this conference will bring national and international speakers to the Isle of Wight to share their knowledge and expertise. Around one third of our speakers are neurodiverse themselves, a further third are parents or carers of neurodiverse people, and all of our speakers are leaders within their fields.
The conference will be free to attend, but you must book your place. There will be a range of presentations and workshops on the day. Individual sessions will be bookable on the day. Due to constraints on numbers we are unable to guarantee places on any specific session, but there will be several streams on the day and there will always be something of interest!



Accessibility

We take our duty to care for all our guests very seriously. We have a number of accessibility facilities at the conference.

  • Colour Communication Badges
  • The Riverside Centre is fully accessible and has a number of disabled parking bays.
  • Creche (subject to space - must be booked in advance, please email neurodiversityiw2019@gmail.com for more details)
  • Breakout space for adults - run by Anxiety Cafe IW
  • Information stands that may be of interest, including universities, third sector organisations and therapists


Venue


We are hosted at the Riverside Centre in Newport, Isle of Wight PO30 2QR. The Riverside includes a number of facilities

  • Lunch can be provided for a charge (must be booked at registration on the day)
  • Tea, coffee and soft drinks will be available to purchase from the bar
  • Free Parking (subject to space - further chargeable parking is nearby)
  • There is a bus stop less than 5 minutes walk away from the venue
  • The Riverside Centre is a non-smoking venue.

Transport & Accommodation

The Isle of Wight can only be reached by boat. Popular ferry services include:
Wightlink Ferries, which sail from Portsmouth and Lymington
Red Funnel, which sails from Southhampton.

From here you can get a bus or taxi to Newport
Buses
Southern Vectis
Taxis
Ryde Taxis Leader Cabs

Buses Guide The Number 9 starts at Ryde Esplanade (the closest bus stop to Ryde Pier), and gets to Newport Bus Station in approximately 40 minutes. The Number 7 starts at Yarmouth Bus Station (for the Lymington to Yarmouth ferry), and gets to Newport Bus Station in approximately 30 minutes. The Number 5 starts at East Cowes Waitrose (a 2 minute walk from East Cowes ferry terminal), and gets to Newport Bus Station in approximately 25 minutes. From Newport Bus Station, it's about a 10 minute walk to the Riverside Centre, which you can easily get the route to with your smartphone.
